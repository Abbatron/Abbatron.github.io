The Sciences through the IB

MYP

In the MYP, all three sciences are assessed the same, so the following criteria apply to all the sciences. Initially, students are taught all three sciences as one subject. Later, they get to choose either 2 out of 3 of the sciences, or to continue doing all three in a condensed format, known as Integrated Sciences.

  • Criterion A - Knowing and Understanding. For this criterion, students must be able to show and use their scientific knowledge to solve problems in both familiar and unfamiliar situations. Students must also be able to use their knowledge to make informed decisions, and scientifically supported judgements.
  • Criterion B - Inquiring and Designing. This criterion aims to make students capable of carrying out scientific investigations independently. Here, students should be able to propose a problem or come up with a question to be tested by a scientific investigation, come up with a testable hypothesis, explain it using scientific reasoning, and design scientific investigations. Student must also be able to explain the different variable and how they can be manipulated and measured.
  • Criterion C - Processing and Evaluating. Students must be able to analyse the results of their investigations and others'. Students must be able to compile and effectively display collected data, and interpret and explain it as well. Students should also be able to evaluate the hypothesis, and see how valid it is, based on how the experiment turned out. Finally, students must be able to relect on their method, and suggest extensions or improvements to it.
  • Criterion D - Reflecting on the impacts of science. This criterion is based off of students learning and displaying the ability to analyse and predict the impacts of science on various problems, and the factors that are affected by, or affect, the problem. Students should also be able to explain in what ways science can be used and applied to solve different problems.

DP

Even in the DP, for at least the subjects of Physics, Biology, and Chemistry, the methods of assessment are similar enough to be grouped together. The following methods of assessment are the same in structure for all three of the above sciences, but the content itself differs.

  • Paper 1 - Paper 1 consists of 30 multiple choice questions for SL students, and 40 for HL students.
  • Paper 2 - Paper 2 consists of two sections, with several questions each. These questions usually supply the student with information in the form of graphs or tables, and then ask the student to answer various sub-questions throughout the course of the question, and then finally summarise the results. There is a second section, where there will be a choice of two questions for SL students, or a choice of three, for HL students. HL students must complete 2 out of the three options, while SL students only need to complete one.
  • Paper 3 - Paper 3 consists of data- and practical-based questions, along with some extended answer questions.
  • The IA - The IA, or the Internal Assessment is an assessment designed to test students' ability to research, act upon that research, and analyse their results and process. Essentially, students should perform an experiment, completely independently, using the full scientific method. Finally, students must complete a write up, on their investigation, which should be 6-12 pages long.
