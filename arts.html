The Arts throughout IB

The Arts that a student can choose from in the IB curriculum are Visual Arts, Drama, Music, Design, and Food Tech. These are all very different themes, but the way in which they are taught is essentially the same, with the same types of assessment and grading, with the aim being the production of some kind of product in all of the aforementioned subjects. Of course, the products in themselves vary widely, but the essential process the IB encourages students to follow remains similar enough that all the criteria for these subjects can be explained as one set of criteria, rather than 5. For the subjects that differ, I will provide that criterion as well.

MYP

  • Criterion A (Design and Food Tech) - Inquiring and analysing. This criterion is all about understanding the problem presented to the student. Students will be expected to select the target audience for their product, develop a detailed design brief for their product, and identify the problem and potential solutions. To do this, they are expected to do thorough research, including comparing existing products and solutions to the problem to get ideas and information.
  • Criterion A (Arts) - Knowing and understanding. This criterion, requires the student to have thorough knowledge of the art form they are studying at the time. The student must be able to demonstrate sufficient knowledge of the art form, as well as needing to be able to use their knowledge to make decisions in regards to the creation of an art piece of that style.
  • Criterion B - Developing ideas. This criterion is all about planning for the creation of the product. The student will need to create detailed design specifications, create separate design ideas, choose and justify one, and develop accurate and detailed diagrams for their chosen final solution.
  • Criterion C - Creating the solution. This criterion is where the student actually creates the product, putting together their new skills and knowledge from the previous two criteria to be able to create on cohesive product that fulfills the design brief. The students will need to be able to create a logical plan, follow the logical plan well enough to create the intended product, which functions as intended. Students also need to demonstrate excellent technical skills during this process. If changes were made to their design specifications, the student will need to be able to justify those changes. Finally, the student needs to present the final solution.
  • Criterion D - Evaluating. This criterion is about being able to reflect on the process of creation up to this point. Students first need to design relevant testing methods to test their product, evaluate their final product with their design specifications and explain if and how the solution could be improved. Finally, the students need to be able to explain the impact of their product on their target audience.

DP - The arts

In the DP, the arts are examined in the following ways.

  • Comparative Study - Students are asked to compare different pieces of art by different artists. This task explores different objects, artifacts and artworks from different time periods and cultural contexts.
  • Process Portfolio - For this task, students are asked to submit a portfolio of selected artworks from their 2-year course which can evidently show how they experimented, learned, and used various different styles of art.
  • The Exhibition - Finally, students are asked to submit a collection of completed artworks from their exhibition for assessment. The pieces should showcase their progress and understanding of the use of different methods to create ideas and convey those ideas through their chosen art form.

DP - Design

In the DP, design technology is assessed through four main criteria.

  • Analysis of a design opportunity. - This criterion asks the student to analyse the different aspects of a design problem, such as the target audience, potential solutions, and how the problem affects the target audience.
  • Conceptual design. - This criterion is all about planning and conceptualising a design for a solution to the problem assessed in the previous criterion. The student should be able to plan and get ready to execute a detailed plan to create a solution to the problem given.
  • Development of a detailed design. - This criterion is the execution of the plan to create the selected final design, the solution to the given problem. The student should be able to follow the plan and design brief created in the earlier criteria.
  • Testing and evaluation. - This criterion is about testing the solution. The student should design effective testing methods to allow them to analyse the effectiveness of the solution in the context of the given problem. The student should be able to ensure that the product functions as intended, and fulfills the design brief to the satisfaction of the target audience.
