Individuals and Societies through the IB

Individuals and Societies subjects include both History and Geography. In the MYP, they are both assessed similarly, but they diverge in the DP.

MYP

  • Criterion A - Knowing and Understanding. This criterion is about students initially just showing their knowledge of individuals and societies, usually through correctly answering questions about them.
  • Criterion B - Investigating. This criterion requires students to showcase and develop their research skills, both collaboratively and singly.
  • Criterion C - Communicating. For this criterion, students need to be able to organize and display collected data in a variety of media and presentation types.
  • Criterion D - Thinking Critically. This criterion requires students to use their critical thinking skill in the context of different problems. This includes things like analysing sources and using information to make considered decisions.

DP - Geography

  • Paper 1 - This paper is 1.5 hours for SL and 2.25 hours for HL. There is one structured question and one extended answer question. There is a choice between two different extended answer questions.
  • Paper 2 - Here, there are three structured questions, based off an infographic or some other visual stimulus, along with one extended answer question, again from a choice of two. This exam lasts for 1.25 hours for both HL and SL.
  • Paper 3 (HL) - This paper lasts for 1 hour. The student can choose between three extended, two-part questions to complete.
  • The Internal Assessment - This makes up 20% of the student's grade. It consists of one written report that includes information collecting, analysis, and evaluation based on a fieldwork question from any appropriate syllabus topic.

DP - History

  • Paper 1 - Paper 1 is one hour long. It is based around four sources, upon which the student must answer 4 questions on. The first question judges the students' comprehension of the source, the second asks the student to evaluate one of the sources' values and limitation, the third asks the student to compare two of the sources, and the final question is an analytical essay question.
  • Paper 2 - Paper 1 is a 1 hour and 30 minute long paper. For this, students must choose two topics from a given list, and answer one essay question each.
  • Paper 3 (HL) - Paper 3 is a 2 hour and 30 minute long paper. Students need to choose 3 essay questions out of a given 36, and answer all three.
  • The IA - The Internal Assessment for History is an in-depth investigation of a historical event of interest to them. The students should clearly state a research question, undertake and clearly represent an investigation, and then effectively review their process, and what they learnt.
