Global Contexts

Global Contexts are important parts of the MYP curriculum. They are essentially different ideas that can be linked to different parts of learning, and are what the MYP units are based around. They are only used in MYP, as in PYP, a different system is used, called trans-disciplinary themes, that help set up the global contexts.

  • Identities and Relationships - This global context is based all around the idea of how people can affect other people things through their actions, and how they in turn can be affected by other people and things. It also explores the relationships between friends, families, and communities. Its PYP equivalent, or the trans-disciplinary theme associated with it, is Who am I? Who are we?.
  • Personal and Cultural Expression - This global context is based around helping students learn the ways in which they can express themselves, their culture, and their emotions. It also encourages students to expand their creativity and reflect on their past experiences. Its PYP equivalent, or the trans-disciplinary theme associated with it, is How we express ourselves.
  • Fairness and Development - This global context discusses the different situations people and places are in. The students will explore their responsibilities to their community, and how to resolve problems peacefully. The PYP equivalent to this global context, or the trans-disciplinary theme equivalent to it, is Sharing the planet.
  • Scientific and Technical Innovation - This global context is all about the ways humans interact with their environment and each other, and the exploration of the ways that science is used in different situations. This global context also explores the impacts, both positive and negative, of scientific advances and their potential applications. The trans-disciplinary theme associated with it is How the world works.
  • Globalisation and Sustainability - This global context is about helping students become more internationally-minded and learning about the different ways humans stay connected, both locally and internationally. It also encourages students to explore the different impacts of the global interconnectedness of modern society. The trans-disciplinary theme associated with it is How we organize ourselves.
  • Orientation in Space and Time - This global context encourages students to look into how different points of history influence the modern day, and how things like location affect people in different ways. The PYP equivalent to this global context, or the trans-disciplinary theme equivalent to it, is Where we are in space and time.
